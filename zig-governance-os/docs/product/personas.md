# Personas — Zig

## GRC Manager / Compliance Manager — "Owns the program"

- **Context**: the senior-most person responsible for governance at the organization,
  often wearing this hat alongside security, legal, or operations responsibilities.
- **Goals**: a defensible, audit-ready program; visibility into maturity without chasing
  spreadsheets; the ability to report status to leadership and to customers in plain terms.
- **Frustrations**: can't get a straight answer to "are we ready for our SOC 2 audit"
  without manually cross-checking several documents; governance work competes for time
  with their primary job.
- **What Zig gives them**: Mission Control as a single source of truth, an explainable
  governance score they can report upward, and Executive Reporting that produces
  audit-ready artifacts without a manual compilation exercise.

## Risk Analyst / Compliance Analyst — "Does the daily work"

- **Context**: the person who actually maintains the risk register, control library, and
  evidence on a day-to-day basis.
- **Goals**: a clear list of what needs attention today; a fast way to create or update
  assets, risks, controls, and evidence without re-deriving framework mappings by hand.
- **Frustrations**: framework mapping is tedious and error-prone when done manually;
  context-switching between a spreadsheet, a framework PDF, and an evidence folder.
- **What Zig gives them**: the Asset/Risk/Control/Evidence/Task Workspaces with
  framework-aware fields built in, and the Health Advisor surfacing exactly what's
  incomplete instead of requiring a manual audit of the register.

## Consultant — "Runs programs across multiple organizations"

- **Context**: an independent or boutique GRC consultant managing governance programs for
  several client organizations at once.
- **Goals**: move fast across clients without losing context; produce consistent,
  professional deliverables per client; demonstrate clear progress to justify ongoing
  engagements.
- **Frustrations**: every client's program tends to live in a different ad hoc format,
  making it hard to reuse work or compare progress across engagements.
- **What Zig gives them**: multi-organization access from one account, the AI Command
  Center to stand up a new client's program quickly, and Scenario Workspace forking/cloning
  to reuse a structure across similar clients.

## Small Business Owner / Founder — "Needs this to just work"

- **Context**: not a GRC professional; needs a credible governance posture because a
  customer security review, an investor, or a partner is asking for one.
- **Goals**: get to "yes, we have a program" with minimal specialized knowledge required;
  understand, in plain language, what's missing and why it matters.
- **Frustrations**: governance frameworks are written for GRC professionals, not founders;
  doesn't know what an "asset inventory" or "control" is supposed to contain.
- **What Zig gives them**: the Guided Project Builder and AI Program Generator to produce
  a complete starting program from a short intake, plus Health Advisor explanations written
  in plain terms rather than GRC jargon.

## Auditor — "Reviews, doesn't build"

- **Context**: an internal or external auditor reviewing the organization's governance
  posture, typically with read-focused access.
- **Goals**: a clear, evidence-backed trail from each framework requirement to the controls
  and evidence that satisfy it, without needing to chase down loose files.
- **Frustrations**: governance artifacts are usually scattered, inconsistent in format, and
  hard to trace back to a specific requirement.
- **What Zig gives them**: the Auditor role with scoped read access, and Executive
  Reporting / Portfolio Artifacts (asset inventory, risk register, control library, gap
  assessment, readiness report, governance summary) generated directly from the live data
  model rather than assembled by hand.
