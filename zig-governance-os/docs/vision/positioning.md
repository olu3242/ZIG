# Positioning — Zig

## Ideal customer profile (ICP)

- Organization size: 10–250 employees.
- Stage: pre-first-attestation through early multi-framework compliance (e.g. has SOC 2,
  now pursuing ISO 27001; or has none yet and a customer/investor is asking for one).
- No dedicated, full-time GRC department — governance is owned by a security lead, a
  founder, an ops lead, or a fractional/consulting GRC professional.
- Industries with above-average governance pressure relative to team size: SaaS, fintech,
  healthtech, and any vendor selling into enterprise customers with security review
  requirements.

## Primary users

- **GRC Manager / Compliance Lead** — owns the program, needs visibility and a defensible
  audit trail.
- **Risk Analyst / Compliance Analyst** — does the day-to-day work of maintaining assets,
  risks, controls, and evidence.
- **Consultant** — runs governance programs across several client organizations and needs
  to context-switch between them without losing continuity.
- **Founder / Small Business Owner** — usually not a GRC professional, needs Zig to do most
  of the heavy lifting and explain itself in plain language.
- **Auditor** — a reviewer, not a builder; needs read access to a clean, evidence-backed
  trail rather than edit access to the program itself.

## Competitors and adjacent tools

- **Heavyweight enterprise GRC suites** (e.g. the Vanta/Drata/Secureframe class of
  compliance-automation tools, and larger legacy GRC platforms): strong at evidence
  collection and integrations, generally weaker at explaining *why* a program is
  structured the way it is, and built for teams that already know what their risk register
  should contain.
- **Spreadsheets and document templates**: the default for most organizations under 50
  people today; fully manual, no scoring, no AI assistance, no enforcement of relationships
  between assets, risks, controls, and evidence.
- **Generic project-management tools repurposed for compliance** (task boards, wikis): can
  track individual to-dos but have no concept of a framework, a control, or a governance
  score.

## Differentiators

1. **AI-generated starting programs**, not blank templates — a new project begins with a
   real, framework-aware draft of assets, risks, and controls instead of an empty form.
2. **One connected data model** instead of disconnected modules: every record traces
   Organization → Project → Asset → Risk → Control → Framework Requirement → Evidence →
   Task → Report.
3. **An explainable governance score and Health Advisor** that runs continuously, rather
   than a report generated only on demand.
4. **Frameworks as metadata**, supporting ISO 27001, SOC 2, NIST CSF, CIS Controls, HIPAA,
   and PCI DSS simultaneously on the same underlying records, instead of one framework
   bolted onto the product at a time.
5. **Built for the practitioner doing the work**, not only the auditor reviewing it
   afterward — zero empty states, guided next actions, and one-click remediation on every
   gap the Health Advisor finds.
