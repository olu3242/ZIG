# Executive Leadership Lab: Build and Deliver a Board-Ready Report

## Scenario
GovSec (`docs/scenarios/GOVSEC.md`).

## Inputs
- GovSec's governance score, open recommendations, and NIST CSF control status
- Executive Leadership track Lessons 01-05 (all five)

## Tasks
1. Decompose GovSec's governance score and translate the top risk into business language.
2. Structure a board report: executive summary, score trend, top risks, recommendations, ask.
3. Build a 12-month roadmap closing GovSec's highest-priority gap.
4. Deliver the report verbally in under 10 minutes to a mock board (ZARA as Hiring Manager/Mentor).

## Deliverables
- Board Report (full) + a recorded or transcribed 10-minute delivery with Q&A.

## Scoring Rubric
| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Score decomposition is accurate and business-language translation avoids both extremes (alarmist/dismissive) | 25% |
| Report structure omits compliance/audit-only detail appropriately | 20% |
| Roadmap sequencing respects real dependencies (e.g. evidence before audit readiness) | 25% |
| Delivery stays within 10 minutes and handles at least one hard question honestly | 30% |

## AI Feedback Rules
ZARA reviews as **Hiring Manager**: scores delivery the way a board would react, and as
**Mentor**: coaches specific phrasing improvements rather than just flagging weaknesses.
