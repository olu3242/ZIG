# Governance Hierarchy

## Purpose
Teaches the learner the standard reporting and authority chain in a governance program —
who sets direction, who oversees, who executes, and who carries out day-to-day work. This
is the foundational structural diagram every governance track lesson builds on before
introducing committees, RACI, or decision rights.

## Structure (content spec, not a rendering implementation)

```
Board
  |
  v
Executive Leadership
  |
  v
Governance Committee
  |
  v
Management
  |
  v
Employees
```

| Level | Owns |
|---|---|
| Board | Ultimate fiduciary oversight, risk appetite approval, strategic governance direction |
| Executive Leadership | Translating board direction into program strategy, resourcing, executive accountability |
| Governance Committee | Cross-functional policy review/approval, escalation routing, governance program operation |
| Management | Implementing controls and policies within their function, day-to-day risk ownership |
| Employees | Following policy, reporting issues, executing controls at the task level |

Governance Committee → Management → Employees) requested for the visual standard. No new
asset name is introduced; this is the elaboration of the existing library entry, and the
library's "Committees" and "GRC roles" map to "Governance Committee" and
"Management/Employees" respectively.
