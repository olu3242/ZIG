# 02_TRANSLATING_RISK_TO_BUSINESS_LANGUAGE

## Objectives
- Translate a `RiskSeverity`/`RiskAssessment` into business impact language (revenue, customers, regulatory exposure).
- Avoid both over-alarming and under-stating risk to a board.
- Connect a risk treatment decision to a business tradeoff a board can actually evaluate.

## Business Context
A board doesn't act on "high severity" — it acts on "this could cost us our largest
customer." This lesson builds that translation skill explicitly.

## Scenario Mapping
GovSec (`docs/scenarios/GOVSEC.md`) — translate its open "Formalize Quarterly Board
Reporting" recommendation into a business-risk framing.

## Framework Mapping
Trains on `Risk`/`RiskAssessment` data reframed for a non-technical audience.

## Diagram Requirements

- Over-alarm vs. under-state risk framing comparison

## Knowledge Check
1. Why is "high severity" insufficient framing for a board-level risk conversation?
2. What's the risk of under-stating a critical risk to leadership?
