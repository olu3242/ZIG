# 04_FINDINGS_AND_CORRECTIVE_ACTIONS

## Objectives
- Write an audit finding with observation, criteria, cause, and effect.
- Distinguish a finding from an observation (severity and required action differ).
- Draft a corrective action with an owner and target date, tied back to a control/task.

## Business Context
This is where audit work becomes actionable for the org being audited — a finding without
a corrective action is just criticism, not governance improvement.

## Scenario Mapping
HealthBridge (`docs/scenarios/HEALTHBRIDGE.md`) — write the finding for its incomplete PHI
access logging and a corrective action tied to a new `Task`.

## Framework Mapping
Trains on the finding-to-`Task` link (Universal Governance Model: Evidence → Task) and
escalation paths described in `docs/scenarios/HEALTHBRIDGE.md`.

## Diagram Requirements
- Finding Escalation Process diagram
- Finding-to-corrective-action-to-task linkage diagram

## Knowledge Check

2. Why must every corrective action have both an owner and a target date?
